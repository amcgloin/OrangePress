Find something to do!

Redlands offers a lot to its residents! But it can be hard to find the group for your passions and interests. I made this site to help you find the people to bike with, read with, or do whatever else you like to do—or want to like to do!—in town. If you want to add additional events or places, you can email me at aidancmcgloin@gmail.com.

Places and things

Athletics

  • Flowstone Climbing
    • Rock climbing gym at 1411 W State Street. Open 8am-10pm weekdays, 10am-9m weekends. $85 monthly. $22 day pass.
  • The Wolf's Den
    • A ninja warrior/obstacle course racing gym with yoga, HITT, a weight center, a chase tag center, knife throwing and pre-planned exercise routines designed by active coaches. Has activities for children. Membership rates are dependent on frequency of attendance and program. Has different programs at different times of the day. Located at 1930 S E St, San Bernardino, CA 92408.
  • YMCA of the East Valley
    • Redlands' YMCA is located at 500 E. Citrus Avenue, downtown. They offer group aquatics and exercise classes for all ages.
  • One Jiu Jitsu Redlands
    • Brazilian Jiu Jitsu Academy at 310 Alabama St. Evening classes. Monthly membership.

Games

  • Board Game Nights—Board Game Paradise
    • Board Game Paradise hosts game nights every Tuesday and Thursday at their downtown location at 109 E State St.
  • Chess in the Park
    • Board Game Paradise hosts chess matches every Sunday at noon at Ed Hales Park, downtown next to their shop. They provide boards, but you can bring your own.

Culture

  • SoCal in Film series
    • The AK Smiley Public Library hosts a movie showing at 2 p.m. every third Saturday. The showings are held in the Contempory Club, which sits behind the Lincoln Shrine.
  • Friends Book Clubs
    • The Friends of the A.K. Smiley Public Library host three book clubs. The Eclectic Readers meet every 2nd Tuesday at 7 p.m. The Third Wednesday Book Club meets at 2:30 p.m. The Fourth Monday Book Club meets at 11 a.m. Contact information for the leaders of the clubs and their current book is available online.

    • Youth events

    • Preschool reading time
      • 30-minute long readings at the AK Smiley Library. 10:15 a.m every Tuesday.
    • Pajama story time
      • Pajama-friendly children reading time at AK Smiley Library. 7:00-7:30 p.m every Wednesday.

    Youth Groups

    • Troop 11

      • Boy Scout troop that goes on camping trips. Meets 7:00 p.m. every Wednesday. Ages 12-17. Yearly dues and uniform required.
